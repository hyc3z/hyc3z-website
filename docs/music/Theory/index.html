Harnessing Space and Time: Introduction to Melody and Two-Voice Counterpoint​

counterpoint 对位

  1. 第一對位：單個音符間的對位（Note agianst note）；

  2. 第二對位：兩個音符與一個音符間的對位 （Two notes against one）；

  3. 第三對位：一組音符（泛稱，包含三個、四個或六個等音符構成的一組）與一個音符間的對位 （Four notes against one）；

  4. 第四對位：延留音對位（Notes offset against each other）；

  5. 華彩對位（Florid counterpoint，將以上四種綜合運用的對位手法）

6 代表主音在最低音上面6度，第一转位

4 6 代表主音在最低音上面4度，第二转位

Most of the time, you should double the bass note somewhere above in the Tenor, Alto, or Soprano voice

ROLE OF VOICES: In general, the SOPRANO should serve as the melody (avoid awkward leaps, too much repetition, and too much pattern in the soprano voice), the ALTO and TENOR serve as harmony voices (finding common tones and stepwise movement from chord to chord) and the BASS serves as the bassline, usually leaping around from chord root to chord root or moving more melodically (MELODIC BASS) when inversions and non-chord tones are in play.
